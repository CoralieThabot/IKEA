We’ve been gathering insider knowledge on some great cities in the UK – so you can bank on a bank holiday like no other. Experience Edinburgh like never before, navigate your way to a new side of Newcastle and look out for local treasures in London. No one does it like the locals.

Mari Vanna
Make yourself comfortable and get ready to wine (or vodka) and dine at Grandma's house in old Russia.
Wilton's Music Hall
This grade 2 listed building is the oldest surviving music hall in the world – an extraordinary place to see live music and world-class productions. Encore!
Twenty Nevern Square
A beautifully restored Victorian town house, with a mix of Eastern and Western design - an absolute charmer (and cheaper than you might think).
The Gardener's Cottage
Seasonal cooking. Social dining. Something different. This historic building is smack bang right in the middle of Edinburgh's world heritage site. Lovely.
Inchcolm Island
Explore a secret island just a short boat ride from the Forth Bridges, and have a wander around the ruins of its 12th century Augustinian Abbey.
The Glasshouse
This 5* iconic luxury hotel has cracking views of the city, a 2-acre roof garden and floor to ceiling windows in every bedroom. Swanky.
The Curry Train
All aboard! Place your order as you travel through the Northumberland countryside, then tuck in when you reach the award-winning The Valley restaurant.
The Victoria
Take a detour 85ft beneath the city's streets. The tunnel runs 2.25 miles from the north of the city down to the River Tyne at Ouseburn.
Jesmond Dene House
Set in a grand stone Arts and Crafts estate on secluded grounds, this fancy hotel does the best afternoon tea on the Tyne.
